# AfterWord

**Your personal library of ideas.**

Every book changes us in small ways. Yet most of those ideas are eventually forgotten.

They remain buried in Kindle exports, scattered notes, screenshots, notebooks, and forgotten documents—captured, but rarely revisited.

AfterWord was built to change that.

Rather than treating highlights as something to collect and forget, AfterWord helps you build a living library of everything you've learned through reading. It's a place where books, highlights, notes, and reflections can exist together and continue to grow long after you've finished the final page.

Import your reading history, explore your library, revisit meaningful passages, and uncover connections across everything you've read.

## Your Reading, Reconnected

AfterWord brings together highlights from across your reading life into a single searchable library.

Currently supported imports include:

* Kindle MyClippings.txt
* Kindle App HTML Exports
* KOReader Clippings.txt
* Libby CSV Exports

Working on adding:
* Apple Books
* Kobo Reader
* Readwise Exports
* and more!


As your collection grows, AfterWord enriches books with metadata, organizes your highlights, and creates new ways to explore the ideas you've accumulated over time.

## Revisit What Matters

One of the most valuable ideas in a book often arrives at exactly the wrong time.

AfterWord helps surface those forgotten insights through daily revisits, making it easier to reconnect with ideas that once resonated with you.

## Search Across Your Entire Library

Search by:

* Books
* Authors
* Highlights
* Keywords
* Concepts

Instead of remembering where you read something, simply remember the idea itself.

## Discover Connections

The Knowledge Map visualizes relationships across your reading history, revealing patterns and themes that emerge across books, authors, and subjects.

Ideas that seemed unrelated when you first encountered them often become part of a larger conversation when viewed together.


## Reading Is Only The Beginning

The value of a book doesn't end when you finish reading it.

The most important ideas deserve a place where they can continue to be discovered, revisited, and connected to everything else you learn.

That's what AfterWord is built for.

## Why you should take it from me?

I built this mainly for myself, I read across a lot of formats and want to revisit my books without figuring out where I read it and clicking through mulitple menus before finding a list of my annotations. And that's not even talking about library books, those ones are gone. 

Since building this, I personally use this extensively and really enjoy revisiting older books I've read from 7-8 years ago and seeing how my thoughts have changed on that.

I've read about 52 books this year and it's only June 2026 so I really needed a way to keep em all straight. I actually love using this, and if you're a reader then I hope you will too!
